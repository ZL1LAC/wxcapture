Satellites

This site publishes data captured from multiple satellites. You can see a larger version of the NOAA and Meteor images by clicking on the image.

You will find lots more images from these satellites via the captures page link at the top of the page.

A 79° pass by the NOAA 18 satellite with a visible light image captured over New Zealand and the South Pacific, with cyclone Harold just about to hit Vanuatu with 240kmph winds on the 6th April 2020. A 62° pass by the Meteor M-2 satellite with a visible light image captured over New Zealand and the South Pacific, on the 11th February 2020. The black lines are due to a software bug on the satellite. An SSTV (Slow Scan TeleVision) image broadcasted by the ISS (International Space Station) in August 2019 to commemorate the life of Owen Garriott who pioneered the use of amateur (ham) radio from space.
